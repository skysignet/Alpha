A New Heraldry

For millennia, the signet ring served as a seal of authority. It was an imprint of the individual pressed into wax and stone. Whether it belonged to a pharaoh or a merchant, the signet carried the weight of a name into the world. It was a symbol of sovereignty.

Each SkySignet is an original composition by Jesse Sky. The design, the calculation, and the arrangement of every element on the face are authored works.

SkySignet evolves this tradition. Where signets once marked inherited power, this one recognizes a deeper origin. Each ring encodes the precise arrangement of the planets at the moment of your arrival. It is a fixed record of the heavens rendered in precious metal.

The design is a composition of degree and shadow. It is legible as a starmap and readable as astrolore. This is an heirloom of the self. It is a beautifully weighted object that carries the cosmos on its face.

No two are the same. There is only one of you. There is only one of these.

✦ The Face ✦

The Orrery

The face of the ring is a geocentric model of the sky. It is a precise astronomical instrument rendered in relief, capturing the heavens from your exact coordinates at the minute of your arrival.

The Foundation: The Nodal Axis
Every SkySignet is oriented along the North and South Nodes. In astronomy, these are the points where the moon's orbit crosses the ecliptic. On the ring, they form a continuous line of direction. When worn, the ring integrates with your hand, the finger pointing toward the North Node.

The Architecture: The Houses
The zodiacal compass is set within this axis. The twelve houses are then calculated, dividing the sky into the specific sectors that governed the horizon at your birth. These houses provide the geometric framework for your personal map.

The Alignment: The Seven Luminaries
Within this architecture, the Sun, the Moon, and the five visible planets are placed at their true astronomical degrees. This creates a unique orbital composition that can be read by an astronomer and recognized by a seeker.

✦ The Tradition ✦

Patterns in the Stars

While the ring is built on astronomical precision, its language is that of astrolore. For millennia, Babylonian, Greek, and Vedic observers have looked to these same celestial positions to recognize patterns in human character and timing.

This is the intersection of math and myth. You are not just wearing a calculation of planetary gravity; you are wearing a lineage of observation that spans four thousand years. Whether you follow the Western Tropical or the Vedic Sidereal tradition, the ring serves as a physical bridge between the motion of the planets and the story of the individual.
